# 1. Strategic summary

GTC Garage should be built as a content-commerce brand, not a generic dropshipping store. The competitive advantage is trust from real projects: cars, motorcycles, campers, tools, accessories, repairs and restorations. Shopify provides the commercial infrastructure while the brand grows through useful, entertaining and credible video content.

## 1.1 Business model

| **Layer**  | **Role**                                                           | **Initial model**                                             |
|------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| Content    | Build trust and demand through YouTube, Instagram and TikTok.      | Organic video, Shorts/Reels, project pages.                   |
| Commerce   | Sell tools, accessories and kits connected to videos.              | Shopify webshop with EU dropshipping suppliers.               |
| Operations | Avoid inventory at launch while keeping delivery times acceptable. | EU-depot suppliers with automated order and tracking sync.    |
| Growth     | Move from dropshipping to owned bestsellers later.                 | Small stock batches, 3PL or branded bundles after validation. |
| Premium    | Sell restored builds or custom projects later.                     | Lead-generation pages, not normal add-to-cart products.       |

## 1.2 Positioning


> **Positioning sentence**
>
> GTC Garage is an automotive content brand and shop for restoration projects, garage tools, car care, camper accessories, motorcycle accessories and products used in real builds.


- Do not compete as a generic automotive marketplace.

- Compete through project-based trust, video demonstrations and curated products.

- Start with universal, non-safety-critical products that can be tested and filmed.

- Use dropshipping only for EU-depot products with acceptable lead times, tracking and returns.

- Move proven products into owned stock once sales data exists.

## 1.3 Launch assumptions

| **Assumption**   | **Decision**                                                                                                  |
|------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Company base     | Sweden, selling first to Sweden and selected EU countries.                                                    |
| Inventory        | No inventory at launch. Dropshipping from EU depots first.                                                    |
| Platform         | Shopify first, custom theme. Headless Shopify only after traction.                                            |
| Brand language   | English for international reach; Swedish and German can be added later.                                       |
| Initial products | Garage tools, detailing tools, winter accessories, camper accessories, motorcycle accessories and organizers. |
| Excluded early   | Safety-critical car parts, complex fitment parts, questionable electronics, chemicals without documentation.  |

# 3. Platform architecture

## 3.1 Recommended setup


> **Recommended technical path**
>
> Start with Shopify plus a custom Shopify theme. Use Shopify for checkout, products, orders, taxes, payments, sales channels and supplier apps. Build custom/headless only after content and product-market fit are proven.


| **Component**    | **Start setup**                        | **Later upgrade**                                        |
|------------------|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Commerce backend | Shopify                                | Keep Shopify unless a very strong reason appears.        |
| Frontend         | Custom Shopify theme                   | Headless Shopify with Next.js/Hydrogen after traction.   |
| Checkout         | Shopify checkout                       | Keep Shopify checkout for trust and reliability.         |
| Supplier sync    | Dropshipping apps connected to Shopify | Custom API/PIM if direct B2B suppliers become important. |
| Content          | Shopify pages/blog + YouTube embeds    | Custom project hub / content platform later.             |

Use this as the operating model:

> YouTube / Instagram / TikTok / SEO -\> GTC Garage website -\> Shop the Build page -\> Shopify product or bundle -\> Shopify checkout -\> Dropshipping app -\> EU supplier -\> Customer delivery -\> Review and email follow-up

# 5. Product and supplier strategy

## 5.1 Initial product categories

| **Category**      | **Good launch products**                                                    | **Why it fits**                                        |
|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| Detailing         | Brushes, microfiber towels, wash mitts, applicator pads, wheel brushes.     | Visual, easy to demonstrate, low fitment risk.         |
| Garage tools      | Trim tools, magnetic trays, socket organizers, tool rolls, work mats.       | Naturally used in videos and builds.                   |
| Garage storage    | Wall racks, shelves, tire racks, storage bins, small workbench accessories. | Fits the Garage pillar and has strong project use.     |
| Winter automotive | Ice scrapers, snow brushes, windshield covers, trunk mats, winter kits.     | Strong Nordic angle from Sweden.                       |
| Camper/vanlife    | Storage, lights, organizers, 12V accessories with proper documentation.     | Supports future camper repair and restoration content. |
| Motorcycle        | Storage, cleaning, tool rolls, covers, simple maintenance accessories.      | Supports bike project content without complex fitment. |

## 5.2 Products to avoid in phase 1

- Safety-critical parts: brakes, steering, suspension, airbags and structural components.

- Vehicle-specific fitment parts requiring complex make/model/year/engine-code data.

- LED headlight conversions or lighting without clear E-mark/type approval where required.

- Chemicals, oils, coatings and fluids without SDS, CLP and compliance documentation.

- Electrical or power tools without reliable CE documentation, manuals and supplier accountability.

- Jacks, lifting equipment or heavy machinery without strong certification and supplier support.

## 5.3 Supplier shortlist

| **Supplier/platform** | **Use case**                                                               | **Decision rule**                                                |
|-----------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Syncee                | Product discovery and Shopify-integrated dropshipping suppliers.           | Use if EU warehouse, order sync and tracking sync are available. |
| AppScenic             | Automated product import, stock/price sync, automatic orders and tracking. | Use for a controlled test assortment, not mass import.           |
| Spocket               | US/EU supplier discovery for accessories and tools.                        | Use only if EU shipping and margin are acceptable.               |
| GOGETTERS             | European supplier app for Shopify with automated processes.                | Good candidate for EU-focused product tests.                     |
| dropXL / vidaXL       | Larger garage/storage items, benches, racks and organizers.                | Use selectively; avoid generic catalogue dumping.                |
| Direct Nordic B2B     | KGK/MEKO/PeBe/local wholesalers later.                                     | Approach after traction for better margins and quality.          |

## 5.4 Supplier approval checklist

- Warehouse location in the EU or Nordic region is confirmed.

- Average delivery times are acceptable for Sweden, Denmark, Finland, Germany, Netherlands, Belgium and Austria.

- Shopify integration supports product import, stock sync, order sync and tracking sync.

- Supplier can provide neutral packaging or blind shipping where possible.

- Return address and return procedure are clear before products go live.

- Product documents are available for tools, electrical items, machines, chemicals and regulated accessories.

- Supplier SKU, margins, shipping costs and return costs are recorded in the product sheet.

- Samples are ordered and checked before products are used in video content.

## 5.5 Product approval workflow

| **Step** | **Action**                                                                    | **Pass/fail criteria**                                        |
|----------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1        | Find product through supplier platform or B2B contact.                        | Relevant to GTC Garage pillars and video content.             |
| 2        | Check warehouse, delivery, stock sync and return rules.                       | EU depot, trackable shipping, clear returns.                  |
| 3        | Calculate margin after VAT, shipping, payment fees and estimated return rate. | Meets target margin before ads.                               |
| 4        | Request compliance documents where relevant.                                  | Docs stored and product risk acceptable.                      |
| 5        | Order sample.                                                                 | Delivered on time with acceptable packaging.                  |
| 6        | Test product in garage/video context.                                         | Quality good enough to recommend.                             |
| 7        | Approve for Shopify and content.                                              | Product page, photos, video, tags and supplier data complete. |

# 7. Operations

## 7.1 Order flow

The dropshipping order flow should be automated, but manually monitored during the first months.

> 1. Customer places an order on Shopify.
>
> 2. Shopify captures payment and creates the order.
>
> 3. Dropshipping app forwards the order to the supplier.
>
> 4. Supplier ships from an EU depot.
>
> 5. Tracking number returns to Shopify.
>
> 6. Customer receives tracking email.
>
> 7. After delivery, the customer receives a review request.
>
> 8. Support issues are tagged by supplier, product and reason.

## 7.2 Customer support setup

| **Support area** | **Setup**                                                                                     |
|------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Email            | support@gtcgarage.com                                                                         |
| Response goal    | Reply within 1 business day during launch.                                                    |
| Tags             | Late delivery, damaged, wrong item, return request, refund, product question, supplier issue. |
| FAQ              | Shipping, returns, product compatibility, tracking, warranty, product documents.              |
| Tone             | Direct, practical, honest and solution-oriented.                                              |

## 7.3 Return flow

> 1. Customer submits return request or emails support.
>
> 2. Check whether the request is inside the applicable withdrawal/return period and whether exclusions apply.
>
> 3. Send return instructions and return address.
>
> 4. Supplier or return location confirms receipt.
>
> 5. Refund or replacement is processed according to policy and law.
>
> 6. Tag the order with the reason so product and supplier quality can be measured.

## 7.4 Supplier KPIs

| **KPI**                          | **Target / action**                                               |
|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Average dispatch time            | Track weekly. Remove products with repeated delays.               |
| Average delivery time by country | Publish conservative delivery estimates by region.                |
| Out-of-stock errors              | Should be rare if stock sync works. Remove unreliable products.   |
| Tracking accuracy                | Tracking should be available and sent back to Shopify.            |
| Defect/damage rate               | Escalate products with repeated quality issues.                   |
| Return rate                      | High return rate triggers product-page or product-quality review. |
| Gross margin after shipping      | Keep products only if margin survives real costs.                 |

# 8. Compliance and risk controls


> **Important operating rule**
>
> For automotive and tools, do not list products only because they are available in a feed. Each product needs a basic risk, documentation, shipping and margin check before it goes live.


## 8.1 VAT and OSS

Because the business is based in Sweden and sells to consumers in other EU countries, VAT and OSS should be reviewed with a Swedish accountant before launch. Configure Shopify tax settings carefully, keep country-level sales records, and define when OSS reporting will be used.

- Use a Swedish accountant with e-commerce and OSS experience.

- Record sales by customer country and tax rate.

- Do not rely only on platform defaults for tax compliance.

- Keep supplier invoices, customer orders, refunds and shipping data organized from day one.

## 8.2 Consumer returns

The webshop should include clear return and withdrawal information before purchase. EU online consumers usually have a cooling-off period for distance purchases. The exact implementation, exceptions and wording should be validated with legal/compliance support.

- Create a clear return policy page before launch.

- State who pays return shipping where legally allowed.

- Make return address and process clear.

- Do not sell products if the supplier return process is unclear.

## 8.3 Product safety and documentation

| **Product type**               | **Minimum control**                                                              |
|--------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| General consumer products      | Supplier, responsible economic operator and product safety information recorded. |
| Electrical items / lights      | CE documentation, manuals, warnings and supplier traceability checked.           |
| Tools / machinery              | CE/conformity documents and manuals checked where relevant.                      |
| Automotive lighting/components | E-mark/type approval checked where applicable.                                   |
| Chemicals / coatings           | SDS, CLP labels, shipping restrictions and local language requirements checked.  |
| Safety-critical parts          | Avoid in phase 1 unless specialist compliance and fitment process is in place.   |

## 8.4 Product compliance fields

| **Field**          | **Example value**                                 |
|--------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Supplier           | Syncee supplier / AppScenic supplier / direct B2B |

| Product risk level | Low / medium / high                               |
| CE required        | Yes / no / unknown                                |

| E-mark required    | Yes / no / unknown                                |
| SDS required       | Yes / no                                          |
| Return address     | EU address confirmed                              |
| Sample tested      | Yes / no                                          |
| Approved for video | Yes / no                                          |

# 9. Launch roadmap

## 9.1 First 90 days

| **Period** | **Focus**              | **Key outputs**                                                                       |
|------------|------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Weeks 1-2  | Foundation             | Domains bought, handles claimed, Shopify opened, email set up, brand basics created.  |
| Weeks 3-4  | Shopify MVP            | Homepage, navigation, policies, analytics, email signup and base theme ready.         |
| Weeks 5-6  | Products and suppliers | 3-5 supplier platforms tested, 30-50 products shortlisted, 10-20 samples ordered.     |
| Weeks 7-8  | First content          | Intro video, first product tests, first Shop the Build template, first products live. |
| Weeks 9-12 | Soft launch            | YouTube and social posting, email list, first sales, supplier performance monitoring. |

## 9.2 Month 4-6

- Remove weak products and unreliable suppliers.

- Create bundles around products that convert.

- Build SEO guides for Garage Setup, Winter Car Care, Camper Restoration Starter Tools and Detailing Starter Kits.

- Approach better Nordic/EU B2B suppliers using early traction data.

- Test small paid campaigns only on proven products or proven landing pages.

- Prepare first branded merch or GTC starter kit.

## 9.3 Month 6-12

- Move bestsellers into small owned stock if margins justify it.

- Consider 3PL storage in Sweden, Germany, Netherlands or Poland for speed and margin control.

- Develop a stronger project hub or headless Shopify if content complexity requires it.

- Start Builds for Sale pages for finished restored/custom vehicles.

- Explore direct supplier agreements and private-label bundles.

- Build a consistent YouTube series with recurring vehicles and characters/projects.

# 11. Later: restored builds and custom projects

When GTC Garage starts selling restored vehicles, campers or motorcycles, treat them as a separate premium sales process. Do not sell them like simple Shopify products with a normal add-to-cart button.

## 11.1 Build page structure

- Build name, project code and vehicle type.

- Full video walkthrough and project playlist.

- Before, during and after photos.

- Work completed and parts/products used.

- Known issues and condition report.

- Inspection, registration and ownership documentation where applicable.

- Price indication or POA.

- CTA: Request build details, book a viewing or reserve interest.

## 11.2 Sales process

> 1. Use Shopify/page content for presentation and lead capture.
>
> 2. Use a dedicated inquiry form, not standard checkout.
>
> 3. Prepare legal contracts, warranty position and condition reports.
>
> 4. Use clear photos, documents and test-drive/inspection process.
>
> 5. Use legal and insurance advice before selling as a business.
